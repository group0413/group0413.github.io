CRISPR-Cas9 technology enables the simultaneous editing of numerous genes. Multiplexing is a capability that allows for more intricate genetic alterations. Depending on the desired result and the complexity of the genetic modification, researchers can select to edit a single gene or use multiplexing.

Common delivery methods include viral vectors, such as lentivirus or adenovirus, which can efficiently deliver the CRISPR components into cells. Non-viral methods, such as electroporation or lipid-based transfection, can also be used for certain applications.

CRISPER-Cas9 can be used not only for gene editing but also for gene regulation. Instead of introducing DNA breaks, researchers can utilize a modified version of Cas9 called HIFICas9, fused with transcriptional activators or repressors. This approach, know has CRISPERa (CRISPER activation) and CRISPERi (CRISPER interference), simultaneously allows for precise control of gene expression without alternation DNA sequence.
